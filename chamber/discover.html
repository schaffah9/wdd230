The City

Avellaneda is a port city in the province of Buenos Aires, Argentina. It has a population of around 400,000 people. Avellaneda is located within the Greater Buenos Aires metropolitan area, and is connected to neighboring Buenos Aires by several bridges over the Riachuelo River.

Geography

Avellaneda is one of the 135 districts of the Province of Buenos Aires. It is one of the most important districts of the Province, not only because of it's location but also for it's rich history, it's cultural and sports heritage and it's economic developments. With it's traditional string of bridges over the Riachuelo, Avellaneda is the entrance to the southeast sector of Greater Buenos Aires. The city's main avenue crosses the entire city from North to South and is one of the busiest communication routes, along with other main roads. Through the Buenos Aires-La Plata highway, Avellaneda connects with the La Plata, capital of the Province, and also with the large touristic centers of the Atlantic Coast.

Industries

Avellaneda is one of the foremost wholesale and industrial centers of Argentina. Dock Sud

The Port of Dock Sud is located on the shores of the Rio de la Plata about 7 km south-southeast of the Port of Buenos Aires and about 49 km west-northwest of the Port of La Plata in Argentina. The Port of Dock Sud was established to lessen the congestion in the Buenos Aires port and to take advantage of direct rail connections with the Port of La Plata. A great part of the the national economy's merchandise enters and leaves through the port making it the second busiest in the country.

National Capital of Football

In 2020, Avellaneda was declared the National Capital of Football. Two of the most important Argentine football clubs, Independiente and Racing, are located in Avellaneda. The city became the third in the world (after Milan and Montevideo) to be home to two world championship teams when Independiente won the 1973 Intercontinental Cup.
